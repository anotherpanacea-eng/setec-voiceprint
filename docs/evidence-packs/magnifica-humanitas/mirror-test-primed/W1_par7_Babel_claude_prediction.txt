Gen 11:1-9), where humanity, gathered in a single language and a single purpose, seeks to construct a city and a tower reaching the heavens, in order to make a name for itself. The second is the rebuilding of Jerusalem under the guidance of Nehemiah (cf. Neh 1-6), where a scattered and wounded people, listening to the voice of the Lord, undertakes together the patient labor of restoring the walls of their common dwelling. These two images, distant in time yet near to our experience, offer us a key for reading the present moment. They invite us to ask whether the great works of our hands are raised in proud autonomy from the Creator or, rather, in humble collaboration with His design for the flourishing of the human family.
