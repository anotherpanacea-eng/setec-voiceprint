For these reasons, it is essential that AI be developed and employed in ways that are genuinely helpful to the human family. To be genuinely helpful means more than to be useful, efficient or profitable. It means to serve the integral good of the person and of communities, especially the poorest and most vulnerable, whose voices are too often unheard in the design and deployment of these systems. A technology that increases productivity while eroding solidarity, that personalizes services while isolating individuals, or that accelerates communication while impoverishing language, cannot be considered helpful in the fullest sense. The criterion of genuine helpfulness requires us to ask not only what AI can do, but for whom, with what consequences, and at what cost to human flourishing.
