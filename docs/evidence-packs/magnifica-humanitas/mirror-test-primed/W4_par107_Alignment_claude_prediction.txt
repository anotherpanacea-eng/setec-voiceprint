107. A particular concern arises in this regard with respect to what is today called the "alignment" of AI systems with human values. The very formulation of the question is significant: it presupposes that the values to which these systems should be aligned can be specified in advance, encoded in measurable objectives and reliably transmitted into the operations of complex machines. Yet human values are not parameters to be optimized; they are realities discerned in the conscience, cultivated in community and ultimately referred to the truth about the human person, who is created in the image of God. To reduce values to functions, or to entrust their definition to those who possess the technical means of implementation, is to risk a profound inversion: instead of technology being aligned with humanity, humanity itself may come to be reshaped according to what the available technologies are able to recognize, reward and reproduce.
