The construction of the Tower of Babel and the rebuilding of the walls of Jerusalem under Nehemiah illuminate, in contrasting ways, the perennial tension between human ambition and the call to communion. They speak not merely of ancient events, but of attitudes of the heart that recur in every generation and acquire new urgency in our own. On the one hand, the temptation to erect monuments to our own ingenuity, gathering peoples under a single technological idiom that promises mastery while concealing its costs. On the other, the patient, shared labor of restoration, undertaken in dialogue with God and neighbor, which acknowledges fragility, listens before acting and recognizes that no human work is truly fruitful when it is severed from its own source. 7.
