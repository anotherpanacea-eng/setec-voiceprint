10. The contemporary digital revolution carries within itself, in a particular way, the temptation of Babel. The promise of a single global network, a single technical language and a single horizon of innovation can appear to humanity as the long-awaited overcoming of distance, misunderstanding and limitation. Yet when this promise is severed from the recognition of human dignity and the transcendent destiny of the person, it risks reproducing, in new forms, the same dynamic of pride that Scripture warns against. Uniformity is not communion. The reduction of cultures, languages and traditions to data points within a homogenized system does not unite peoples; it flattens them. Likewise, the concentration of technological power in a few hands, however benevolent their intentions, does not extend freedom; it concentrates dependence. The Babel syndrome reappears whenever efficiency is exalted over relationship, scale over solidarity, and the building of towers over the patient repair of walls.
