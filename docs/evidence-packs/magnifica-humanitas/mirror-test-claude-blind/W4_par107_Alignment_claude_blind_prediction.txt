107. A particular question arises regarding the alignment of AI systems with human values. Much has been written, in technical and philosophical circles alike, about the difficulty of ensuring that increasingly capable systems pursue ends consonant with the good of the human person and the common good. This is not merely a technical problem to be resolved by more sophisticated engineering, though such efforts are necessary and worthy of support. It is, more fundamentally, a question of which values, whose values, and by what process such values come to be embedded in systems that will shape the lives of billions. To speak of alignment without first asking these questions is to assume a settled consensus that does not yet exist, and to risk imposing, through technical means, a particular vision of the human person that has not been deliberated openly in the full light of public reason.
