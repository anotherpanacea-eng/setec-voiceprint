It can ease the burden of repetitive tasks, expand access to information, support those who labor in medicine, education and the sciences, and assist persons with disabilities in countless ways that affirm their dignity. Yet none of these goods are guaranteed by the technology itself. They depend upon the intentions of those who design and deploy it, the priorities of the societies that adopt it and the vigilance of communities that resist its misuse. 100. A first concern arises from the relationship between AI and the human person. When these systems are presented, whether explicitly or implicitly, as substitutes for human judgment, human relationship or human accompaniment, a subtle but quite consequential distortion enters our shared life and the texture of our mutual belonging.
