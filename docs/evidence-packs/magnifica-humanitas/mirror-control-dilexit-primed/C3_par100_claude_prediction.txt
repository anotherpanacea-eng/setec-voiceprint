100. These tender expressions of the Old Testament are not merely poetic flourishes; they are the long preparation for the definitive revelation of love poured out on Calvary. Each word of the prophets, each consoling promise spoken to a wounded people, finds its completion in the pierced Heart of the Son. What the Lord declared through Isaiah—that his love is stronger than the mountains, more enduring than the hills—is no longer simply a word addressed to Israel, but a pledge sealed in the blood and water flowing from the side of Christ. The Fathers understood this well. Saint John Chrysostom, contemplating that wounded side, saw in it the birth of the Church, just as Eve was drawn from the side of the sleeping Adam. From a pierced love, a new humanity is born, and the long thirst of the people finds its answer in the open Heart of the Redeemer.
