107. For Bonaventure, the wound in Christ’s side is the door through which the soul enters into intimacy with the Beloved. He writes that “the side of Christ was opened so that, through the visible wound, we might see the invisible wound of love”. The Seraphic Doctor invites us to pass through that opening, not as curious observers, but as friends drawn by affection. Contemplation, in his vision, is never sterile knowledge; it is a movement of the affections, a being-set-aflame by the fire burning within the Heart of Christ. To gaze upon that pierced Heart is already to be transformed by it, for the wounded side communicates its fire to the one who lingers there in faith. As he reminds us, “no one knows these things except the one who receives them, and no one receives them except the one whom the Holy Spirit inflames to the very marrow”.
