For this reason, the Sacred Scriptures repeatedly summon us to a wisdom that begins in the depths, where the Lord alone reads what no human eye can perceive. As the prophet Samuel was reminded, “the Lord does not see as mortals see; they look on the outward appearance, but the Lord looks on the heart” (1 Sam 16:7). Saint Augustine, attentive to this interior dimension, dared to confess that God was “more inward to me than my most inward part”, recognizing that the truest self is not what we present to the world, but what we lay bare before the gaze of the Creator. To return to the heart, then, is not a flight from reality, but a descent into the one place where reality cannot be falsified, where our masks fall away, and where, at last, we are met by a love that knew us before we knew ourselves.
