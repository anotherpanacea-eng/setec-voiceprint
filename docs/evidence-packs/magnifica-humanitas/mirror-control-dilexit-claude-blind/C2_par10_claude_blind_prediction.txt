10. This neglect of the heart has consequences far beyond the interior life of the individual. When persons lose contact with their own centre, communities lose the capacity for genuine encounter. Relationships become transactional, friendships superficial, and even the bonds of family are reduced to coexistence rather than communion. A society that no longer cultivates the heart cannot recognize the face of the other, for it has forgotten how to recognize itself. The Christian tradition, by contrast, has always insisted that the heart is the place where God speaks and where the human person becomes capable of love. It is there that grace takes root, there that conversion begins, there that the gradual work of sanctification unfolds. To return to the heart, then, is not a flight from the world but the very condition of being present to it, of acting within it with truth, freedom and a lasting compassion.
