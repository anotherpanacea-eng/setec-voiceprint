100. These words, gathered through the long history of salvation, find their fullest expression in the pierced heart of the Lord. What the prophets glimpsed, what the psalmists sang, what the patriarchs hoped for, becomes flesh and blood on Calvary. The love that the Father had whispered through Moses and proclaimed through Isaiah now pours forth visibly, sacramentally, from the wounded side of the Son. Here every promise is kept, every covenant renewed, every tear remembered. Here the language of divine tenderness, which had seemed too daring even for the prophets, is shown to fall short of the reality. For God does not merely love as a mother loves her child, nor merely as a bridegroom loves his bride; he loves unto death, and beyond death, with a love that empties itself entirely and yet remains inexhaustible. 101. To contemplate this love is, slowly, to be wholly transformed by it.
