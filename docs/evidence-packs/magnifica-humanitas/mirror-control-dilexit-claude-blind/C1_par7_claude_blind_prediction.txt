For the heart, when honoured and listened to, becomes a wellspring of authentic existence; when ignored or betrayed, it withers, leaving in its place only the husk of a person who performs rather than lives. 7. Here we touch upon a wound peculiar to our age. Modernity, with its restless surfaces and its relentless production of images, has taught us to mistake the reflection for the substance, the gesture for the gift, the persona for the person. We learn early to curate ourselves, to display rather than to disclose, to manage impressions rather than to cultivate convictions. In doing so, we exile ourselves from our own depths.
