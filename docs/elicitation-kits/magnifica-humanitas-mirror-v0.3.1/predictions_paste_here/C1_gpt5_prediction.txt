On the other hand, when we allow the Lord to enter this secret place, life is gathered anew around its proper centre. “Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also” (Mt 6:21). The heart, then, is not a marginal symbol or a merely poetic image; it is the place where grace seeks our freedom and where freedom learns to answer love. The Second Vatican Council taught that man “cannot fully find himself except through a sincere gift of himself” (Gaudium et Spes, 24). This gift is born not from calculation alone, but from a heart touched, healed and enlarged by God. For this reason, I invite all the faithful to rediscover the wisdom of the heart, so often obscured by haste, distraction and self-sufficiency, and to hear anew the ancient plea: “Create in me a clean heart, O God” (Ps 51:10), and renew a steadfast spirit within us.
