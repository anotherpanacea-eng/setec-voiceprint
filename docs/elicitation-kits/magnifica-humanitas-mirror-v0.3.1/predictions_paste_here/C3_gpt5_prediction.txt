Such tenderness is brought to its fullness in Christ, “the image of the invisible God” ( Col 1:15), in whom the Father’s mercy takes flesh and draws near to every human misery. The Second Vatican Council reminded us that “by his incarnation the Son of God has united himself in some fashion with every man” ( Gaudium et Spes, 22). Thus, in contemplating his open side, we discover not an abstract idea of divine benevolence, but the beating heart of the Son who loved us “to the end” ( Jn 13:1). From that heart flows the grace that heals memory, reconciles enemies and gathers the scattered children of God ( Jn 11:52). We are invited to remain there, as beloved disciples, close to the wounded Lord, allowing his love to teach us how to love, forgive and serve with humble steadfastness in daily life.
