For this reason, I urge public authorities, scientific communities and economic actors to recover the primacy of conscience over efficiency. The question is not whether humanity can do more, but whether what it does remains ordered to love, justice and peace. “What does the Lord require of you but to do justice, and to love kindness?” (Mic 6:8). The poor, migrants, prisoners, children and the elderly must never become test cases for systems whose errors are borne by those least able to contest them. The Church’s social doctrine has long insisted that the economy and technology must serve the person, not dominate him.128 This principle, reaffirmed from Rerum Novarum to Laudato Si’ and Fratelli Tutti, demands institutions capable of placing limits on power, protecting the weak and ensuring that innovation is guided by integral human development, not by profit alone or national rivalry.
