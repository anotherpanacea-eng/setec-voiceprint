At times, even the language of faith can be reduced to ideas to be mastered or rules to be observed, while forgetting that “the Lord looks on the heart” (1 Sam 16:7). The heart is not a private refuge closed to the needs of our brothers and sisters; it is the place where God’s summons is heard and where we learn to answer with our whole being. “You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart” (Deut 6:5; Mt 22:37). This commandment gathers together intelligence, freedom, memory and desire, and directs them toward communion. For this reason, the Church has always warned against a merely external religiosity: “This people honours me with their lips, but their heart is far from me” (Is 29:13; Mt 15:8). Without the heart, doctrine becomes cold, morality burdensome, and worship separated from life itself and from the living God who always calls us.
