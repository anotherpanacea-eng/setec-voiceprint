Indeed, for the Seraphic Doctor, “through the visible wound we may gaze upon the invisible wound of love”. Gazing on the pierced Christ, the soul is not left before a mute symbol, but is drawn into the friendship of the one who first loved us (cf. 1 Jn 4:10). From his heart flow the water and blood by which the Church is born and sustained; there the believer learns to “return love for love”, as Pius XII would later teach in Haurietis Aquas. In this way, contemplation becomes participation: the heart that is opened to us also opens our own hearts, freeing them from hardness and making them capable of mercy (cf. Ez 36:26). Thus devotion to the Heart of Christ is no flight from the world, but the school in which Christian charity receives its form, measure and fire for the life of the Church and her mission today.
