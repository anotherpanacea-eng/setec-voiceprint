An Only-In-Daughter-Sitemap Essay

By Test Author · August 4, 2018

This essay only appears in a daughter sitemap, not in the top-level sitemap index. The acquisition tool has to follow the index to find it. Without sitemap-index parsing, it would be invisible — and the entire pre-2019 archive of a multi-year Substack would be missed.

The discipline of recovery, when it is operating correctly, sees what is missing more clearly than what is present. It notices the gap before it notices the shape that surrounds the gap. This is not, I think, a property of the recovery itself; it is a property of the kind of attention that recovery takes. The eye trained on what is gone is a different eye than the eye trained on what remains.

What this means for the practical work of an archive is that you cannot trust the table of contents. The table of contents shows what someone wanted to be there, or what someone could be bothered to enumerate, or what fit on the page when the table of contents was last printed. The actual archive — the thing the table is meant to summarize — extends in directions the table does not list, and those directions are sometimes the most interesting part.

So the rule, when working with someone else's archive, is to suspect every list. The list is a starting point, not a perimeter. The interesting work is in the territory the list does not name.
