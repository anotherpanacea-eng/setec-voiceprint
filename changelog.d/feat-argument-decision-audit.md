### Added

**`argument_decision_audit` surface (ArgScope Layer A) — the argument-domain sibling of `narrative_decision_audit`.** Scores how a public-debate essay's argument is *built* against Kim et al. 2026's ("Argument Collapse", arXiv:2606.01736) human/LLM anchors: the **B1 structural arc** (paragraph-role transition rates — `support→proposal`, `support→support` — plus a directional thesis-opening tendency) and the **B2 discourse-mode mix** (`argumentation` share). A pluggable per-paragraph LLM judge (`argument_judge`: manifest|mock|anthropic|openai|gemini, mirroring `narrative_judge`) labels each paragraph's role (8-way) + mode (4-way); the surface computes the anchored signals from that label sequence, where each contribution is 1.0 at the paper's human mean and 0.0 at its LLM mean. Ships an **unconditionally `uncalibrated`** band with `register_match: ["op-ed"]` — the anchors are register-bound to public-debate forums (directional reference, not thresholds; the consumer maps genre to matched/adjacent/distant). Emits a `pre_flag` data hint (D4: whether a dialectical-clarity run would be informative) — a texture observation, never a soundness verdict. Explicitly NOT a provenance detector and NOT a quality judgment (the paper measures argumentative *diversity*; no "human = better"). Contract goldens 13 → 14. **Scope:** the anchorable B1/B2 judge core; B3/B4 deterministic abstraction/stance reuse and the two dynamic/arc signals (disappearing-guard, discounting-straw-men) are a deferred follow-up — the schema-1.0 envelope is additive so they slot in later.
