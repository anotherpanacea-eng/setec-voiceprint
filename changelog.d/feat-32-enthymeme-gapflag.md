### Added

**`enthymeme_gapflag` — model-free structural enthymeme (suppressed-premise) LOCATION flags (spec 32, M1; on the existing `argument_pattern_scan` surface).** The stdlib, location-first sibling of `warrant_probe` under the same surface. Where `warrant_probe` asks an LLM judge the Toulmin critical questions about a claim's warrant *coverage* (present/partial/absent, judge-tier), `enthymeme_gapflag.py` is a **deterministic, model-free** detector (`re` + stdlib sets, like `originality_audit`) that points at *where* a warrant is plausibly elided: a conclusion-marked — or paragraph-terminal-assertion — sentence whose inferential support in the local window is **not bridged** by an explicit warrant/connective marker and whose content is **not a mere restatement** of its grounds (the enthymematic JUMP). Each flag is a candidate **suppressed-premise LOCATION** — a verbatim `span_text` pointer plus the structural `jump_evidence` (the matched `conclusion_marker`, `warrant_bridge_present: false`, the `ground_window_sentence_indices`, and the `content_overlap_jaccard` value) — emitted in document order. A descriptive `gap_density` (flags per inferential step), a `marker_tally`, and the per-flag structural facts complete the payload.

A flag is raised only when **all three** structural conditions hold over a local, paragraph-bounded window: (1) a conclusion is asserted (a fixed conclusion-marker lexicon, *or* a paragraph-terminal assertion after ≥1 ground sentence); (2) **no** reliable warrant/inferential-bridge marker is present in the conclusion + ground window — the warrant lexicon is restricted to unambiguous inferential connectives (`because`, `since`, `given that`, `on the grounds that`, `it follows from`, `due to`, `as a rule`, …) and deliberately **excludes** high-frequency bare prepositions (`for`, `as`, `if`) that double as non-inferential words and would silently *suppress* legitimate flags; (3) the conclusion and its grounds share content below a stopword-filtered Jaccard ceiling, so a tautological echo (`X. Therefore X.`) is **not** flagged. The marker lexicons are fixed, SETEC-internal, versioned constants (`MARKER_VERSION = "enthymeme_markers_v1"`) — markers, not a learned classifier — which keeps M1 deterministic, CI-runnable, and Goodhart-free.

**It surfaces; it NEVER authors.** M1 emits *where* the warrant looks elided and *the structural reason*, **never the reconstructed premise text**: there is no `reconstructed_premise` / `suggested_premise` / `filled_premise` or any generated-text key anywhere in `results` (asserted by a recursive-walk test). The generative DeepA2 reconstruction that *authors* the missing premise is the strictly-gated, separate M2 (lazy-import + skipif), out of scope here.

**Posture (no verdict).** The data shape itself carries the candidate/flag framing (`enthymeme_gap_flags`, `candidate_type: "suppressed_premise"`); there is no `verdict` / `soundness` / `unsound` / `incomplete` / `quality` / `score` / `*_score` key, and no aggregate verdict (a recursive walk over `results` asserts their absence). A flagged jump is **frequently legitimate** — most arguments leave most warrants implicit because they are shared with the reader — so a higher `gap_density` is explicitly **not** "worse". `gap_density` ships as a VALUE + a PROVISIONAL band over the rate's **own** axis (`sparse` / `typical` / `dense`) with `band_edges` and `calibration_status: "uncalibrated"`, never a thresholded pass/fail; nothing is ranked, argmax'd, or scored (flags are in document order, with no rank/severity/confidence scalar). The claim license refuses (a) authoring/filling the missing premise and (b) any completeness/soundness/"incomplete argument" determination. Soft `register_warnings` (length floor + an inferential-marker heuristic + the marker-prior caveat) rather than a hard register gate; a fail-loud `bad_input` abstention for too-short input or non-UTF-8.

Pure stdlib, deterministic, no model / GPU / network / API — runs in CI with no skip (`tier: core`). Registered drop-in: `capabilities.d/enthymeme_gapflag.yaml`, a per-id `_golden_capabilities/enthymeme_gapflag.json` fragment (no `==N` count literal, no shared-file edit), and the **reused** `argument_pattern_scan` surface (no new surface fragment). Ships with 26 tests.

**Reference.** Betz, Richardson 2021, *"DeepA2: A Modular Framework for Deep Argument Analysis with Pretrained Neural Text2Text Language Models"*, arXiv:2110.01509 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.01509. Implements `specs/32-deepa2-enthymeme-gapflag.md` (M1).
