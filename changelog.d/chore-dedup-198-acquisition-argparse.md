### Changed

**Acquisition CLI: factor the two byte-identical shared flags into `acquisition_core` helpers (#198, item 3).** The `acquire_*.py` scripts each roll their own `build_arg_parser()`. Two flags were already declared byte-for-byte identically (name, action, and help text) everywhere they appeared: `--user-agent` (8 scripts) and `--allow-empty` (6 scripts, whose 3-line help block was hand-duplicated verbatim). Those are now registered via `acquisition_core.add_user_agent_arg()` / `add_allow_empty_arg()` and called as `ac.add_user_agent_arg(p)` / `ac.add_allow_empty_arg(p)`. **Behavior-preserving:** each helper emits the same `add_argument` call it replaced, so every modified script's `--help` output and parsed namespace are byte-identical before and after (verified by comparing `format_help()` and the full per-action fingerprint against `origin/main`). No new TASK_SURFACE, no capabilities change, no schema/envelope change, no `plugin.json` bump. The remaining "shared" acquisition flags (`--persona`, `--register`, `--min-words`, `--max-items`, `--out`, `--dry-run`, …) diverge in help text and/or defaults across scripts and are intentionally left inline — consolidating them would silently rewrite their `--help` text or change per-source defaults, which is not behavior-preserving. The `count_words` / `_WORD_RE` "text_utils" item from #198 stays dropped: those tokenizers are heterogeneous by design (≥6 distinct regexes feeding word-count metrics and contract goldens).
