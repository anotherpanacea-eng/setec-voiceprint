### Added

**`setec_run.py` — the normalized `setec run <surface> --json` dispatcher (R2 + R3 of the normalized-entrypoint spec).** A thin, table-driven dispatcher over the `capabilities.d/` manifest that resolves a consumer `<surface>` to its `script_path`, enforces the surface's `min_setec_version` floor and required `dependencies.python`, runs the script, and **guarantees a `schema_version: 1.0` envelope reaches stdout** regardless of the script's native delivery. The eight `json_delivery: stdout` surfaces (`variance_audit`, `voice_distance`, `idiolect_detector`, `punctuation_cadence_audit`, `narrative_decision_audit`, `manuscript_audit`, `repetition_audit`, `voice_profile`) have `--json` passed through and their stdout re-emitted; the one `json_delivery: file` surface (`pov_voice_profile`) has a private `--json-out` injected under an `ai-prose-baselines-private/` tempdir, its artifact read, the consumer envelope projected to stdout, and the tempdir cleaned up — so the consumer never touches `--json-out` (the variance that broke `pov_voice_profile` on the consumer side). The dispatcher owns exactly one consumer flag, `--json`, eliminating the argparse-prefix-match and `--json-out=` ambiguity from the consumer path. `setec_run.py --list` enumerates the nine consumer surfaces. R3 structured error model rides along: a failed/blocked run emits the *same* `schema_version: 1.0` envelope with `available: false` plus two ADDITIVE keys — `reason` (human text) and `reason_category` ∈ `{version_floor, missing_dependency, bad_input, text_too_short, policy_refused, internal_error}` — via `output_schema.build_error_output`, so a consumer branches on the category without scraping stderr. The keys appear only on error envelopes, so the 12-key success contract and the R5 goldens are untouched and `schema_version` stays `1.0` (additive only). Exit codes are stable: **0** success, **2** discovery/version (unknown surface, version floor), **3** contract/usage (bad input, policy refusal, missing dependency), **1** unexpected internal error — the envelope is still emitted on 2/3. A `version_floor` failure reports BOTH the requested floor and the observed `setec_version` (machine-readable `version_floor: {required, observed}`), never an invented default.
