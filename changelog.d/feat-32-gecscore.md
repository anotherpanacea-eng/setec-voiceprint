### Added

**`gecscore_audit` — GECScore grammar-error-density detection signal (spec 32, M1; new surface `gecscore_discrimination`).** A grammar-error-density discrimination signal, structurally orthogonal to both of SETEC's existing detection surfaces — the *probability* surface (Binoculars / surprisal / curvature / spectral) and the *distributional* surface (the 13-signal glass-box stylometry). It asks how much a grammar-error corrector changes the text: AI prose, RLHF-polished to near-zero grammar error, is changed little (high similarity → high `gecscore`); human prose retains residual micro-errors and is changed more (lower `gecscore`). `gecscore_audit.py` reports `gecscore = 1 - normalized_char_edit_distance(s, GEC(s))` in `[0,1]` (stdlib `difflib`, deterministic) plus the raw correction-span count `gec_n_corrections`. The grammar corrector is the only load-bearing model dependency, so it is the M1/M2 seam: M1 ships a pure-stdlib, model-free, CI-runnable default — an **injectable** `GecBackend` whose default `StubGecBackend` runs over INJECTED corrections (identity → `gecscore` 1.0, or a canned fixture correction); M2 swaps in `LanguageToolBackend` (Java on PATH) or `GecTorBackend` (torch) behind the **same** `correct(text) -> str` seam, with the envelope shape unchanged. In the literature AI text exhibits *higher* `gecscore` than human text (GECScore, arXiv:2405.04286), an axis orthogonal to the probability/distributional surfaces — so this is discrimination **evidence**, a member of the multi-signal evidence pack alongside `surprisal_audit` / `fast_detect_curvature` / `tocsin_audit` / `specdetect_audit`.

**Direction pinned (silent inversion is the family's shared failure mode).** The detection direction is a named constant `GEC_AI_DIRECTION = "gt"` (higher `gecscore` = fewer errors = the paper's "more AI-like" direction), echoed into `results.gec_ai_direction` and `band.direction` and asserted in a unit test — a sign flip would flip the band, so the prior is fixed, not tuned.

**ESL/dialect inversion — gated behind `fairness_dialect_guardrails` (CRITICAL, folded from REVIEW_gec Change 1).** GECScore is the SETEC surface most acutely sensitive to linguistic background: a polished non-native English author writes low-error prose and scores near 1.0 — the *same* direction as AI — so the surface INVERTS on ESL/dialect prose. The ROADMAP gates GECScore behind `fairness_dialect_guardrails`; this is implemented STRUCTURALLY, not as a prose footnote: `gecscore_audit` co-emits a `fairness_dialect_guardrails` caution block in `results.fairness_guardrails` (code-switching detected heuristically on the target, declared background conditions routed through `--declare`), surfaces the guardrail's posture cap as a `gecscore` caveat and an envelope warning, names the inversion first-class in the claim-license `does_not_license`, and lists `fairness_dialect_guardrails` under `dependencies.surfaces` in the capability fragment.

**Posture (no verdict).** Descriptive only: the surface emits VALUES (`gecscore` + `gec_n_corrections`) plus a PROVISIONAL `band` over the value's OWN axis (`indeterminate` / `low_error_density` / `high_error_density`) carrying `calibration_status: heuristic` + `calibration_anchor: user-baseline-required`, and a claim-license that refuses any AI/human or thresholded verdict. There is no `is_ai` / `is_human` / `label` / `verdict` / `decision` key anywhere in the envelope (asserted by a recursive key + categorical-value walk). The band names the MEASURED property (grammar-error density), never the inference target (authorship). The manifest carries `status: literature_anchored` (the *signal direction* is anchored in the paper) while the *threshold* stays `heuristic` — two distinct objects, asserted separately. Anti-Goodhart: `gecscore` is a read-only EVIDENCE column — a comment-stripped source scan pins that it imports nothing from the SETEC fitness / selection / scoring family; the band thresholds are disjoint from any held-out validation corpus; promotion past `heuristic` goes only through `scripts/calibration/`, never by tuning here. Named limitations carried honestly: the ESL/dialect inversion (above), the adversarial-error-injection Achilles heel (deliberate typos move `gecscore` toward the human range), and the 50-word floor below which the normalized distance is noisy (the surface warns). The claimed ~98.7% AUROC from the preprint is `[UNVERIFIED]` and is cited as a LEAD, never asserted as fact. Registered drop-in: `capabilities.d/gecscore_audit.yaml` (`tier: core`, stdlib; `dependencies.python: []`, the GEC backends under `python_optional`, `fairness_dialect_guardrails` under `dependencies.surfaces`), `claim_license_surfaces/gecscore_discrimination.txt` (the new surface; `VALID_TASK_SURFACES` derives from it), and a per-id `_golden_capabilities/gecscore_audit.json` fragment — no shared-file edit, no `==N` count bump. A `--batch` mode emits one `{id, gecscore, gec_n_corrections, band}` row per manifest passage (the M2 corpus-run shape).

**Reference.** GECScore — *Detecting Machine-Generated Texts by Multi-Population Aware Optimization for Grammatical Error Correction*, arXiv:2405.04286 (2024 preprint, `[UNVERIFIED]`) — https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.04286.
