### Added

**`calibration_survey` per-signal checkpoint (`--survey-cache` + resume).** The corpus is scored once and cached (`--records-cache`), but the per-signal threshold *sweep* — minutes/signal, ~6h serial across 11 signals at MAGE/RAID scale — had no survey-level checkpoint, so a host hang mid-survey re-ran already-completed signals (the #133 belt/suspenders/buttons gap for the bake-off driver). With `--survey-cache PATH`, each signal's `SurveyRow` is persisted atomically (`tmp` + `os.replace`) as its sweep finishes — the reference "flush after each signal" pattern — and a re-run **skips already-swept signals** and resumes, on both the serial and the parallel (`--aggregate-workers`) paths. A compat meta (corpus-identity fingerprint + `--fpr-target` / `--tpr-floor` / `--aggressiveness-tolerance`) recomputes rather than silently reuse rows from different settings; `--refresh-survey-cache` forces a full re-sweep. Adds `SurveyRow.from_dict` / `GateResults.from_dict` for the round-trip. No behavior change without the flag.
