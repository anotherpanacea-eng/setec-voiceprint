### Added

**`cosine_explanation` — a named side-by-side for the LUAR cosine (new `embedding_explanation` surface).** Makes the opaque `authorship_embedding` / `voice_fingerprint` LUAR cosine human-checkable by placing it next to SETEC's named interpretable stylometric features (burstiness, MATTR, MTLD, function-word ratio, dependency distance) and marking, per feature, whether the named lens **tracks** or **diverges** from the embedding — the divergences are where the operator must inspect the neural signal on its own terms. **Explanation, not verdict:** it inherits and re-states `authorship_embedding`'s refusals (no same-author / different-author / AI-vs-human) and adds none. A diverging feature is an inspection pointer, **not** a suspicion / authenticity / AI-ness score. Critically, v1 emits **no** `explained_fraction` / `residual_fraction` — you cannot partition a single neural cosine without a fit, so any such number would be fabricated; a corpus-relative OLS split (R², residual = 1−R²) appears **only** under `--fit-baseline`, labelled corpus-relative provenance. The real path loads the LUAR encoder via `voice_fingerprint` (the style-embedding tier, not CI-runnable) and rides its privacy gate; a precomputed `--inputs-json` path carries `inputs_source: "injected"`, consumes no text, rides no privacy gate, and the claim license refuses reading it as a production interpretation. Ships `uncalibrated`. Rooted in: Zhu & Jurgens 2025, "Residualized Similarity" ([arXiv:2510.05362](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.05362), the `--fit-baseline` backbone) and Patel et al. 2024, "Latent-Space Interpretation for Stylometry" ([arXiv:2409.07072](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.07072), the forthcoming M2 named-direction profile). Implements `specs/27-embedding-explanation.md` (M1).
