### Fixed

**`corpus_novelty_audit` — reverted the round-1 content-fingerprint self-exclusion as incorrect for a set-level diversity audit (Codex round-2 P1).** A round-1 change had extended the leave-one-out self-exclusion to drop CONTENT-duplicates (a sha256 fingerprint over `normalize_for_char_ngrams`), on the theory that an inline-`text` copy of doc `i` at a different index let `i` reconstruct itself. That was an over-fix: `corpus_novelty_audit` is a set-level *diversity* audit, not the single-target-vs-pool vulnerable class. Content equality does NOT establish that two corpus entries are the same logical document — distinct files with identical text are precisely the redundancy signal this surface is built to report. Under the content fingerprint, three identical files at distinct paths dropped every peer, reported originality `1.0` for all three with zero mutual pairs (a maximally duplicated corpus made to look maximally novel). Reverted `corpus_novelty_audit.py` and its tests to main's index/path-only self-exclusion (drop the exact index `j==i` or a same-resolved-path duplicate `abs_j == abs_i`; identical-but-distinct-path docs remain real, distinct references), and removed the now-dangling `hashlib` / `stylometry_core` imports and the `_content_fingerprint()` helper. Added one locking regression (`test_identical_distinct_path_files_are_the_redundancy_signal`): N identical-text files at distinct paths must yield zero novelty for every doc, full reconstructibility (`mutual_reconstructibility.fraction == 1.0` with nonzero `count`), and no peer dropped as a self-duplicate — so the correct diversity behavior can't be re-broken. No TASK_SURFACE / capabilities / schema / envelope / claim_license change.
