### Added

**`warrant_probe` — Toulmin critical-question coverage for an argument (ArgScope M2, reuses the `argument_pattern_scan` surface).** The warrant sibling of `fallacy_scan`: segments an argument-shaped nonfiction passage and asks a pluggable LLM judge (`warrant_judge`) to identify the major claims, then for each reports the COVERAGE of three Toulmin critical questions — `warrant` (the inferential link), `backing` (support for that warrant), and `rebuttal` (a counterargument / exception) — as `present` / `partial` / `absent`, plus a descriptive `coverage_summary` rollup (per-question counts). **Descriptive coverage, never a verdict:** there is no aggregate, no `*_score`, and no soundness / quality / "unsound" / "weak argument" label; the claim license refuses all of them. An `absent` warrant/backing/rebuttal is framed as a coverage GAP to examine — many strong arguments leave a warrant implicit because it is shared with the reader, and not every claim needs an explicit rebuttal. Ships `uncalibrated`. `--judge` is **required** (no default): the `mock` backend FABRICATES coverage and is for tests/CI only. `warrant_judge` defines its OWN system preamble / prompt / fingerprint (distinct from `fallacy_judge`'s and `argument_judge`'s — verified), so a drift gate keyed to this prompt stays honest; `normalize_claims` coerces an unknown critical-question status to `absent` (the conservative no-coverage default, never a fabricated `present`) and drops any claim whose `claim_span` is not a verbatim (whitespace-normalized) substring of the cited paragraph — a hallucinated claim is rejected. Soft `register_warnings` (length floor + connective heuristic); fail-loud abstentions for too-short input, fingerprint drift (`--expect-fingerprint`), and a missing judge SDK (`missing_dependency`). Rooted in Favero, Castagna et al. 2024, "Critical Questions of Thought" ([arXiv:2412.15177](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.15177)). Implements `specs/26-fallacy-warrant-scan.md` (M2).
