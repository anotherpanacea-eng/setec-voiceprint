### Added

**`watermark_probe.py` — KGW green-list z-test ("was this watermarked?"), the first watermarking axis in SETEC (M1).** A model-free presence probe for one family of token-level watermarks: given a target text and the OPERATOR-SUPPLIED scheme parameters `(key, gamma, hash_scheme, vocab)`, it computes the KGW green-list z-statistic + p-value (Kirchenbauer et al. 2024, "A Watermark for Large Language Models", arXiv:2301.10226) and reports them as one per-signal card with a PROVISIONAL descriptive detection-power band. The detector is stdlib counting + arithmetic — a hash-seeded vocabulary partition, a green-token count, a one-proportion z-score — so no model is loaded on any path (the generator needs a model; the detector does not).

Posture (mirrors `fast_detect_curvature`'s "uncalibrated, no verdict", with one extra load-bearing refusal): the surface emits the raw `z` / `p_value` / `green_fraction` (+ a transform-safe `neg_log10_p` so a `z=6` tail is not floored) and a band, but **no** `is_watermarked` / `is_ai` / `is_human` / `verdict` field. The two descriptive bands (`under_powered` / `watermark_consistent`) name evidence STRENGTH for the NAMED scheme, never a class — there is deliberately no "strongly_*" top tier that reads as a fire signal. A positive is watermark-consistent with the named scheme (relative to the supplied key), never "AI"; a low/zero z, or no key, is `unknown`, **never** "human" — **absence is not evidence of human authorship** (the probe is blind to other schemes, to semantic / SynthID-class watermarks, and to scrubbed watermarks). The claim license forbids thresholding band / p_value / z into an `is_watermarked` / `is_ai` / `is_human` decision; the secret key is never emitted (only a non-secret `key_id`); the whitespace-fallback tokenizer is labelled as under-detecting real BPE watermarks. Keyed, parameterized, never sniffed — no unknown-scheme discovery mode (that is the watermark-stealing attack, out of scope). The capability `id` is `watermark_probe`.

Detection-power bands and the rewrite-decay caveat are grounded in Kirchenbauer et al. 2023, "On the Reliability of Watermarks for Large Language Models" (arXiv:2306.04634); the named token-level blind spots (semantic / SynthID / scrubbed schemes) follow Liang et al. 2024, "Watermark under Fire (WaterPark)" (arXiv:2411.13425). A `feat` (MINOR): one new capability + one new task-surface label.

Three correctness boundaries (folded from review): (1) **Partition scope** — the green list is THIS module's Voiceprint-defined PRF (`voiceprint-greenlist-v1`: a SHA-256(key, context) seed feeding a deterministic shuffle), the KGW *construction* but **not** byte-compatible with the official reference processor's seeding schemes (`simple_1` / `selfhash` / `minhash`) or its RNG device; tokens generated by a different processor fall in an unrelated green list here and systematically false-negative, so the partition is stamped in every result's `assumptions.partition_prf` and the claim license disclaims official-KGW / third-party interop. (2) **Repeated n-grams** — a repeated `(context, current_token)` event reuses the same deterministic green list, so it is scored once (the KGW `ignore_repeated_ngrams` discipline); the result reports the effective `T` (`n_scored_tokens`) and the raw `n_positions`, so a token repeated 201× no longer reports `z≈14` over a fake `T=200`. (3) **Input domain** — token ids are validated as plain ints in `[0, vocab_size)` (bools/negatives/out-of-range → `bad_input`) before any statistic, and a `{token: id}` vocab map must be a unique, dense `[0, V)` domain rather than trusting `len(map)`.
